DA50 RG

Personal Project

Summary

This personal project was a deep-dive into CATIA V5 to strengthen my 3D modeling and surface modeling skills. To build the lifting surfaces accurately, I imported the airfoil points and splines for the NACA 64(2)-415 airfoil (from airfoils.com) using an excel macro that formatted the coordinates for direct curve creation in CATIA. Those airfoils were then lofted to design the wings, winglets, and tail.

Overall aircraft dimensions of the spans, lengths, and key cross-sections were taken from Diamond’s technical specifications on diamondaircraft.com. The propeller geometry was generated using JavaProp from the published specs. The remaining fuselage contours (nose, fairings, and fillets) were estimated by comparing multiple reference photos and matching proportions in CATIA to produce smooth surface transitions.
