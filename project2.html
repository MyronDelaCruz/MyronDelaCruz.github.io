Bottle Cap Sorter

Northern Alberta Institute of Technology

Summary

At N.A.I.T, I was the team leader for my capstone project. Our sponsor created mosaic art from bottle caps and needed to eliminate the time-sink of manually sorting caps by colour. I designed an automated sorting machine that separates large batches of bottle caps via a vertical converyor belt, then presents them one-by-one to a colour sensor. A linear actuator opens a trap door corresponding to the detected color, while the main door holding the bottle cap rotates 90° to allow the bottle cap through. The bottle cap then rolls down the slide and through the opened trap door.

The concept targets up to 19 colours, prioritizes accuracy over speed, and is designed to be modular, maintainable and low-cost for a single maker’s workflow.

What?
  • Design a fully automated machine to sort bottle caps by dominant colour
  • Handle batches of ~1,000–3,000 caps with minimal supervision
  • Target up to 20 colour bins with accuracy higher priority than speed
  • Meet build constraints: < 8 ft² footprint, < 6 ft tall, durable for tens of thousands of cycles
  • Stay within a $500–$1,250 manufacturing budget
How?
  • Designed in Autodesk Inventor with technical drawings
  • Modular sub-assemblies for easy assembly/disassembly and maintenance
  • An optical colour sensor reads the cap's dominant colour while the main gate holds it stationary. Readings are mapped to a target colour bin.
  • A NEMA-17 stepper, driven by an Arduino microcontroller paces the cycle and coordinates the linear-actuated trap doors, where only the selected bin's door opens for each cap.
  • Gate logic: (1) The main door closes to stop a single cap in front of the sensor. (2) After a valid colour is detected, the selected trap door opens via its linear actuator. (3) The main door rotates 90° to release the cap so it rolls down the slide. (4) The trap door closes and the main door returns to the hold position for the next cap.
Results
  • Manufacture-ready concept meeting size, durability and cost constraints
  • Specified a colour sensor (TCS3200) capable of detecting the sponsor’s wide colour range
  • Expected throughput 720–1200 caps/hour with accuracy-first tuning
  • Design package delivered: CAD, BOM, calculations and assembly documentation
