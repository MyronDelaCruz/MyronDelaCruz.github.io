FEA of Saturn V's F-1 Engine (ANSYS Mechanical)

Project – Structural analysis of deformation, stress, & contact separation (gapping)

Cornell University

Summary

This project builds finite-element model in ANSYS Mechanical to assess the bolted flange joint that connects the mid and lower sections of the Saturn V’s F-1 engine nozzle. The model captures contact between parts, bolt pretension, a pressure gradient along the nozzle derived from 1-D gas dynamics, regeneration-channel forces that tend to separate the joint, and thermal strain at 700 °F. Materials are 300-series stainless steel for the nozzle shells and A-286 steel for bolts and nuts. Project specifications taken from the Cornell University Course ENGR2000X: A Hands-on Introduction to Engineering Simulations.

Objectives: determine bolt margin of safety and quantify joint gapping under realistic combined loading. Results show bolt stresses ≈ 80 ksi (below an A-286 capability of ~160 ksi) and a maximum gap of ~0.005 in near the regeneration channel. Design responses include increasing bolt preload, adding a washer, or increasing flange radius to reduce local opening. Convergence was achieved for sequential load steps representing pretension, pressure + channel forces, and thermal loading.

What?
  • Modeled the bolted flange joint between mid & lower nozzle nozzle.
  • Accounted for bolt pretension, thermal strain, and a nozzle-axis pressure gradient (12.17–47.72 psi).
  • Targets: bolt margin of safety and joint gap under combined loads.
How?
  • Geometry: simplified cone built in ANSYS DesignModeler (mid, lower nozzle, 200 bolts + 200 nuts).
  • Mesh: Hex-dominant; body sizing ~0.075 in (bolts/nuts) and 0.3 in (shells), to generate a mesh element size of 26743 which is within the ANSYS student 32000 node limit.
  • Solution: Newton–Raphson with 3 load steps: (1) bolt pretension @ 50% UTS, (2) pressure + regeneration forces, (3) 700 °F thermal strain.
  • Boundary conditions: periodic symmetry; frictionless support at mid-nozzle top.
Results
  • Bolt Von Mises stress ≈ 80 ksi < 160 ksi (A-286 capability) → positive margin of safety.
  • Peak opening gap of ~0.005  near the regeneration channel under combined loads.
  • Future improvements to reduce gap: increase pretension, add washers, and increase the flange radius.

Modeling Notes & Interpretation
  • Three sequential load steps were applied: (1) bolt pretension to clamp the joint, (2) nozzle pressure gradient + regeneration-channel forces that tend to pry the flange apart, and (3) a 700 °F thermal field that drives thermal expansion between nozzle and bolts.
  • Using Saturn V's rocket chamber pressure of 1000 psi and an estimated area ratio along the length of the nozzle, the area-Mach relation was used to find the Mach number and pressure along the nozzle.
  • The pressure was assumed to vary linearly along the nozzle axis. The surface-to-surface contacts capture local separation and load transfer across the flange.
  • The regenearation channels were omitted in the model. The force of the regenerative channel was modeled as two separate forces, each of 1000 lbf. The gas temperature is 700°F, which causes thermal strain.
  • Bolt stress levels remain below the A-286 Steel allowable stress limit of 160 ksi. Peak gap of ~0.005 inches at the regeneration channel highlights where sealing or fatigue risk could rise.
  • To reduce local gap, options include increasing bolt preload, adding washers to spread bearing stress, or increasing flange radius.
  • A Hex-dominant mesh was used to balance accuracy with the student node limit. Future mesh refinement could be done with the MultiZone method.
