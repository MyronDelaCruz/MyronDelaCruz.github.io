Mechanical Engineer in Training, Specialization in Aerospace

I have completed my Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering with an Option in Aerospace at the University of British Columbia. I am registered as an Engineer-in-Training (EIT) with Engineers and Geoscientists British Columbia (EGBC). My primary interests include aircraft design, aerodynamics, propulsion and power systems, as well as MRB engineering and manufacturing. I enjoy the design and analysis of aircraft structures and systems, and have growing experience in using CATIA V5 and SolidWorks for CAD modeling, and ANSYS Fluent for aerodynamic simulations through personal projects. I am motivated to build on these skills and apply them to projects that contribute to innovation and efficiency in the aerosapce industry.

This website provides an overview of various work experiences, projects, and related activities that I have done since the start of my Bachelor's program.

Projects

Unmanned Aerial Fire-Fighting Assistant (UBC)

Designed a robust tether connection that lets a drone autonomously latch to a ground tank and make a quick-connect electrical link. The project focused on the locking hook and pin mechanism, an actuator-driven magnetic plug, and a sealed housing engineered for 3000 lb loads, ±30° alignment, and 150 °C operation. The concept was prototyped and verified with FEA.
